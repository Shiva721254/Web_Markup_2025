Welcome — for Asian expat families

Beatrixpark is a calm, green space in the heart of Almere. This guide is made for expat families—especially newcomers from Nepal and India—who want friendly, practical ideas to enjoy nearby nature. You will find short loops for walking, cycling and skating, plus a page with playgrounds and simple amenities such as benches, picnic tables and waste points. The goal is to make twenty minutes outdoors a weekly routine: a stroll after school, a small picnic at the weekend, or a practice lap for a child learning to ride. These easy moments help you feel at home and meet neighbours along the way.

Start with a short loop that matches your energy. Choose wide paths, keep right on narrow sections, and bring water or a thermos. In spring you may hear birds at dawn; summer offers cool shade under large trees; autumn brings warm colour for photos; winter reveals clear branch shapes and the geometry of paths. Many families enjoy timing the same lap each week and noticing how seasons change the light and sound of the park. Keep a small bag for litter, stay on paths and give wildlife space—careful habits protect nature for everyone.

Use the menu to explore: Nature explains seasonal highlights; Visit (Routes) lists four easy loops with simple tips; Playgrounds & Amenities describes places for children and quiet rests; About us shares the project purpose; and Contact offers a small form for questions or ideas. We use plain language, semantic HTML and responsive CSS so the site works on phones and computers alike.
