Nature through the seasons

Families often notice how the park changes each month. In early spring, bulbs and shrubs brighten the edges and hedges fill with birdsong. Summer brings long shade lines and a soft wind across the grassâ€”perfect for a short reading break on a bench. Autumn glows warm through gold and red leaves; calm days are great for photos near still water. Winter reveals the structure of trees and the geometry of paths, turning a simple lap into a refreshing routine with crisp air and quiet soundscapes.

Explore slowly and teach children to observe without disturbing wildlife. Watch bees and butterflies on sunny borders, look for small fungi after rain, and notice how different trees cast texture across the paths. Bring a small bag for litter, stay on marked paths and keep a respectful distance from animals. These small actions protect habitats, keep the park welcoming, and help kids learn stewardshipâ€”caring for a place they enjoy.

What to look for

  • Flowering borders in spring and mirrored reflections after calm weather
  • Songbirds near hedges at sunrise and before sunset
  • Tall trees offering cool shade for short summer breaks
  • Mushrooms in leaf litter following a day of rain
