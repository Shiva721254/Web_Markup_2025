Who we are & why this site exists

This is a small student website designed to help Asian expat families—especially newcomers from Nepal and India—feel confident exploring Almere’s Beatrixpark. The project began with simple questions: Where do we go with kids after school? Which routes are safe to practise cycling or skating? Are there benches, tables and bins nearby? Our pages turn those questions into routes, tips and friendly guidance.

We keep the site technical-jargon free and follow good web-markup practice: semantic structure, accessible labels, clear link states and mobile-first CSS. If you have a favourite loop, a useful tip or a nice photo, please share it via the contact page. Together we can encourage neighbours to spend time outdoors, live actively and care for local nature. Legal note: Icons and trademarks belong to their owners (©, ®, ™). This site is non-commercial and free (€0); in the UK that’s £0. Quotes appear as "double quotes" where needed, and ampersands use &.

We aim for clean code that passes validators without errors or warnings. The CSS uses relative units, spacing controls and a consistent type scale. Layout uses Grid and Flexbox; the table is zebra-striped with `:nth-child`; and the cookie message appears above all content thanks to `z-index: 9999`. You can view the design screenshots later in the /design folder as mobile.png and pc.png exported from your Web Design prototype.
