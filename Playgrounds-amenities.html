Playgrounds & places to pause

For children, play is how you learn a new country. Beatrixpark offers spaces for different ages: small slides and sand for little ones; climbing frames for young adventurers; and open lawns for ball games and frisbee. Teenagers can practise skating or casual workouts. Adults can take quiet breaks on benches, read a book in shade, or do gentle stretches at the edge of a lawn. Please share paths kindly and keep noise low near resting areas.

Practical details help visits run smoothly. Benches and tables appear near path junctions, making it easy to pause for a snack. Waste bins support a clean environment—pack a small bag and leave spaces tidy. Bring water; public taps may be seasonal, so check signage. Bike racks near entrances keep parking organised. If you plan a meet-up, choose a clear landmark and a time with good light. Families from Nepal and India often bring homemade snacks; consider reusable containers and a blanket for comfort. Small routines like these turn a quick visit into a relaxed tradition.

Checklist for families

  • Benches & tables: spaced along main paths for picnics and rest
  • Waste bins: near junctions and playgrounds—pack out extra litter
  • Bike racks: near entrances for safe parking
  • Shade & sun: pick shade in summer, sunny edges in winter
